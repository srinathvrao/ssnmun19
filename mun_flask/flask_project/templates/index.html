The Committees

Disarmamaent and International Security Council

The first committee of the General Assembly, the UNGA-DISEC is involved with disarmament, global threats to peace and other related international security questions. It considers all disarmament and international security matters within the scope of the Charter or relating to the powers and functions of any other organ of the United Nations. All 193 member states of the UN have equal voting rights in the DISEC. The UNGA DISEC is one of the five committees being simulated at SSN MUN this year.


Human Rights Council

The United Nations Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body which was established by the UN General Assembly to replace the UN Commission on Human Rights. Since its inception in 2006, the primary responsibility of the UN HRC is to ensure the promotion and protection of human rights, by addressing human rights infringements and making recommendations for the same.


Security Council

The United Nations Security Council, touted as the institution's most powerful body, is the organ established by the UN Charter and whose primary responsibility is the maintenance of international peace and security. The council convenes regularly to assess issues to international security and is empowered to impose binding obligations on all 193 member states of the United Nations.


Economic and Financial Committee

The ECOFIN is the second committee of the United Nations General Assembly and a very unique committee that deals with issues relating to economic growth and development, policy questions, global financial partnerships, bailouts and even issues relating to groups in special situations!
