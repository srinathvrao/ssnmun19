From The Secretary General

To prospective delegates and the members of the MUN community,

On behalf of the entire organising committee of SSNMUN 2019, it is my pleasure to announce that this year’s edition of SSN Model United Nations will be held on the 30th, 31st of august and the 1st of September, 2019.

Having historically been home to a culture conducive to the spirit of free-thinking and argumentation, SSNMUN has continually fostered excellence in debate and diplomacy over the years. The members of the MUN community of our institute have always been dedicated to curating a world-class conference experience on our home ground.

While the direct lessons from MUNs are manifold, the indirect outcomes are the most useful lessons as they are the ones that stay with you for a lifetime. The ability to negotiate with people while maintaining diplomacy, dissecting and analyzing complex problems, understanding everyone’s perspective of a problem, and most importantly, to actively listen to friends and foes alike, is what makes MUNs so very valuable.

We live in trying times, when the principles that hold together the very frame-work of our democracy are being held up to scrutiny. I believe platforms such as MUNs will serve as a mechanism for the youngsters of our country to discuss and debate upon topics pertaining to happenings within our country and international geo-political topics as well.

Having been a part of SSNMUN in various capacities such as a delegate, USG, GA etc, I can more than assure you that you can find some of the finest Executive Board Members and highest quality debate coupled with supreme hospitality during the course of the MUN. In conceptualizing SSNMUN 2019, we harbor the earnest hope of being able to carry forward the SSNMUN brand. We look forward to you being a part of this journey, with us.

Hope to host you soon!

Navneeth Venkatesan,
Secretary General,
SSNMUN 19

The Committees

Disarmamaent and International Security Council

The first committee of the General Assembly, the UNGA-DISEC is involved with disarmament, global threats to peace and other related international security questions. It considers all disarmament and international security matters within the scope of the Charter or relating to the powers and functions of any other organ of the United Nations. All 193 member states of the UN have equal voting rights in the DISEC. The UNGA DISEC is one of the five committees being simulated at SSN MUN this year.


Human Rights Council

The United Nations Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body which was established by the UN General Assembly to replace the UN Commission on Human Rights. Since its inception in 2006, the primary responsibility of the UN HRC is to ensure the promotion and protection of human rights, by addressing human rights infringements and making recommendations for the same.


Security Council

The United Nations Security Council, touted as the institution's most powerful body, is the organ established by the UN Charter and whose primary responsibility is the maintenance of international peace and security. The council convenes regularly to assess issues to international security and is empowered to impose binding obligations on all 193 member states of the United Nations.


Economic and Financial Committee

The ECOFIN is the second committee of the United Nations General Assembly and a very unique committee that deals with issues relating to economic growth and development, policy questions, global financial partnerships, bailouts and even issues relating to groups in special situations!
